      <h2>About Me</h2>
      <p>
        I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Iowa, working in the
        Smart Multifunctional Material Systems Laboratory. My research focuses on bio-inspired smart
        materials, artificial muscles, soft actuators, adaptive mechanical systems, active flow control,
        and antifouling surfaces.
      </p>
      <p>
        My work combines mechanical design, materials processing, finite element modeling,
        experimental characterization, real-time sensing, and control systems to develop functional
        soft robotic and adaptive structures. I am particularly interested in translating smart material
        systems into practical devices for robotics, underwater systems, aerospace-inspired flow control,
        and sustainable engineering applications.
      </p>
    </section>

    <section id="research">
      <h2>Research Projects</h2>

      <div class="project-grid">

        <div class="card">
          <h3>Vacuum-Driven Snap-Through Artificial Muscles</h3>
          <p>
            Developed a vacuum-actuated bistable linear actuator that uses snap-through instability
            for fast and efficient actuation. The system achieved approximately
            <span class="highlight">4.1 cm stroke in about 0.3 s</span> while lifting loads up to
            <span class="highlight">0.5 kg</span> at less than 65 kPa vacuum.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="card">
          <h3>Bioinspired Active Vortex Generators for Underwater Flow Control</h3>
          <p>
            Designed artificial muscle-actuated deployable vortex generators for hydrofoils.
            The system improved lift coefficient by approximately
            <span class="highlight">25–30%</span> and reduced drag by about
            <span class="highlight">8–20%</span> at high angles of attack.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="card">
          <h3>Octopus-Inspired Antifouling Smart Skin</h3>
          <p>
            Developed an artificial-muscle-actuated smart skin capable of controlled surface deformation
            for marine biofilm release. The system achieved greater than
            <span class="highlight">90% biofilm release efficiency</span> at approximately 3.2 V.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="card">
          <h3>Deployable Vortex Generators for UAV Applications</h3>
          <p>
            Developed cephalopod-inspired deployable vortex generators powered by twisted spiral
            artificial muscles for low-Reynolds-number aerodynamic flow control and stall-delay applications.
          </p>
        </div>

        <div class="card">
          <h3>Nanomechanical Characterization of PdSe₂</h3>
          <p>
            Used nanoindentation and nanoscratch testing to evaluate hardness, modulus, creep behavior,
            wear resistance, and strain-rate sensitivity of PdSe₂ for flexible electronics applications.
          </p>
        </div>

      </div>
    </section>
