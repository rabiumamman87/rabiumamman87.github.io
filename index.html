About

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Iowa, working in the Smart Multifunctional Material Systems Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Caterina Lamuta. My research focuses on bio-inspired smart materials, artificial muscles, adaptive structures, soft actuators, active flow control, and antifouling surface engineering.

My work combines mechanical design, materials processing, finite element analysis, control systems, experimental mechanics, and multiscale characterization. I have developed artificial muscle-driven systems for underwater flow control, UAV flow control, active biofilm release, and vacuum-driven snap-through actuation. My research has been published in peer-reviewed journals including Advanced Functional Materials, iScience, Robotics Reports, Smart Materials and Structures, and Materials Science and Engineering: B.

Research Projects

Vacuum-Driven Snap-Through Artificial Muscles

Developed a vacuum-actuated bistable linear actuator that exploits snap-through instability for fast and efficient linear contraction. The system achieved approximately 4.1 cm stroke in about 0.3 s while lifting loads up to 0.5 kg at less than 65 kPa vacuum.

Methods: CAD design, Abaqus FEA, FDM printing, Flexible 80A resin, vacuum regulation, NI-DAQ, Arduino control, Keyence laser displacement sensing.

Bioinspired Active Vortex Generators for Underwater Flow Control

Designed artificial muscle-actuated deployable vortex generators for hydrofoils. The device improved lift coefficient by approximately 25–30% and reduced drag by about 8–20% at high angles of attack.

Methods: water-channel testing, flow visualization, thermistor feedback, MATLAB/Simulink, ROS, Arduino, electrothermal actuation.

Octopus-Inspired Antifouling Smart Skin

Developed an artificial muscle-actuated smart skin capable of controlled out-of-plane deformation for active marine biofilm release. The system achieved greater than 90% biofilm release efficiency at approximately 3.2 V.

Methods: PID control, optical microscopy, Keyence laser confocal profilometry, biofilm mechanics, physics-based modeling.

Deployable Vortex Generators for UAV Applications

Developed cephalopod-inspired deployable vortex generators powered by twisted spiral artificial muscles for low-Reynolds-number aerodynamic flow control and stall-delay applications.

Methods: wind tunnel testing, electrothermal actuation, adaptive control, soft actuators, flow-control experiments.

Nanomechanical Characterization of PdSe2

Used nanoindentation and nanoscratch testing to evaluate hardness, Young’s modulus, creep behavior, wear resistance, and strain-rate sensitivity of PdSe2 for flexible electronics applications.

Methods: Hysitron TI-950 TriboIndenter, nanoindentation, nanoscratch, wear testing, mechanical reliability analysis.
